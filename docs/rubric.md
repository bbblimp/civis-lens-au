# Review Rubric

Each criterion is scored from `0` to `5`.

## Score Anchors

| Score | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No credible basis for a positive assessment |
| 1 | Very weak; major flaws or missing evidence |
| 2 | Weak; some merit but substantial unresolved issues |
| 3 | Mixed; workable in parts, with clear tradeoffs |
| 4 | Strong; generally credible with manageable risks |
| 5 | Very strong; highly credible, well-supported, and resilient |

## Criteria

### Clarity

Is the policy intelligible, scoped, and internally coherent?

### Feasibility

Can it realistically be funded, administered, and implemented?

### Evidence Support

Are the central claims backed by credible and relevant evidence?

### Equity Impact

How well does the policy account for distributional effects across groups and regions?

### Implementation Risk

What is the likelihood of delivery failure, gaming, leakage, or harmful side effects?

### Accountability

Are there clear measures, oversight mechanisms, and feedback loops?

### Long-Term Resilience

Is the policy likely to remain functional under changing conditions, incentives, or external shocks?

## Mandatory Narrative Sections

Every review should also state:

- strongest argument in favor
- strongest argument against
- likely unintended consequences
- evidence gaps
- confidence level
- alternatives or design improvements
